Dynamic Adaptive Streaming over HTTP

A while back I wrote a report on dynamic streaming over HTTP (DASH) with a classmate. The report, that was limited to about 8 pages, is linked at the end of this blogpost. The idea behind DASH is to allow video streaming over the internet to adapt the quality of the video to the available bandwidth. Such as the quality of the video is maximized, but the video does not freeze or stall. This is especially useful in high jitter network links such as long range mobile communication or high load networks.
